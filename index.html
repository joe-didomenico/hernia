⚠️ Anytime: Call Your Surgeon / Seek Care If

Fever > 101°F (38.3°C)
Increasing redness, warmth, or drainage
Severe or worsening pain after day 5–7
Inability to urinate
Sudden large, painful swelling
Recovery Progress
+

First 24–48 Hours (Immediate Recovery)

Goals: pain control, protect repair, avoid strain
Activity
Rest, but don’t stay in bed all day
Short walks every 1–2 hours while awake (prevents clots, helps bowel function)
Pain control
Use prescribed medications as directed
Alternate acetaminophen ± ibuprofen if allowed
Avoid NSAIDs if your surgeon said not to
Ice packs
15–20 minutes, 3–4× per day over the groin (wrap ice pack in cloth)
Incision care
Keep incisions clean and dry
Shower usually allowed after 24–48 hours (no soaking or baths)
Diet
Light meals initially
High-fiber foods and plenty of fluids
Stool softener
Start immediately (very important — no straining)
+

Days 3–7 (Week 1)

Goals: gentle mobility, swelling reduction
Walking
Increase distance daily, but no rushing
Activity limits
No lifting greater than 10 lb
No pushing, pulling, or hard bending
Avoid coughing strain — use a pillow to brace if needed
Swelling & bruising
Normal and often worse on days 3–5
Scrotal swelling and bruising are common after bilateral repair
Driving
Usually okay when:
• Off narcotic pain medication
• Able to brake suddenly without pain
+

Weeks 2–4

Goals: gradual return to normal movement
Activity
Light household tasks are OK
Walking and stationary bike are usually allowed
Still avoid
Heavy lifting (greater than 20 lb)
Core exercises, squats, and deadlifts
Pain
Pain should be steadily improving
Occasional twinges are normal
+

Weeks 4–6

Gradual strengthening if cleared
