⚠️ Anytime: Call Your Surgeon / Seek Care If

Fever > 101°F (38.3°C)
Increasing redness, warmth, or drainage
Severe or worsening pain after day 5–7
Inability to urinate
Sudden large, painful swelling

First 24–48 Hours (Immediate Recovery)

Goals: pain control, protect repair, avoid strain
Activity
Rest, but don’t stay in bed all day
Short walks every 1–2 hours while awake
Pain control
Use prescribed medications as directed
Alternate acetaminophen ± ibuprofen if allowed
Avoid NSAIDs if told not to
Ice packs
15–20 minutes, 3–4× per day (wrap in cloth)
Incision care
Keep clean and dry
Shower after 24–48 hours (no soaking)
Diet
Light meals initially
High-fiber foods and fluids
Stool softener
Start immediately — no straining
+

Days 3–7 (Week 1)

Goals: gentle mobility, swelling reduction
Walking
Increase distance daily, no rushing
Activity limits
No lifting > 10 lb
No pushing, pulling, hard bending
Brace with pillow when coughing
Swelling & bruising
Normal; often worse days 3–5
Scrotal swelling/bruising common
Driving
Off narcotics
Can brake suddenly without pain
+

Goals: gradual return to normal movement

Core work, squats, deadlifts
Pain
Should steadily improve
Occasional twinges are normal
