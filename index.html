⚠️ Anytime: Call Your Surgeon / Seek Care If

Fever > 101°F (38.3°C)
Increasing redness, warmth, or drainage
Severe or worsening pain after day 5–7
Inability to urinate
Sudden large, painful swelling
Recovery Progress
+

First 24–48 Hours (Immediate Recovery)

Goals: pain control, protect repair, avoid strain
Activity
Rest, but don’t stay in bed all day
Short walks every 1–2 hours while awake (prevents clots, helps bowel function)
Pain control
Use prescribed medications as directed
Alternate acetaminophen ± ibuprofen if allowed
Avoid NSAIDs if your surgeon said not to
Ice packs
15–20 minutes, 3–4× per day over the groin (wrap ice pack in cloth)
Incision care
Keep incisions clean and dry
Shower usually allowed after 24–48 hours (no soaking or baths)
Diet
Light meals initially
High-fiber foods and plenty of fluids
Stool softener
Start immediately (very important — no straining)
+

Days 3–7 (Week 1)

Increase walking gradually
No lifting > 10 lb
+

Weeks 2–4

Light household tasks only
Avoid core strain
+

Weeks 4–6

Gradual strengthening if cleared
